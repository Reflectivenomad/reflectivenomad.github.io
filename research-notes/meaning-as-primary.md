Meaning as a Primary Motivation
Research Question
Is meaning itself an irreducible motivational system, or does it emerge from combinations of survival, sociality, and other motivational structures?
Competing Possibilities
Position A: Meaning is fundamental.
Humans pursue symbolic significance independently of survival or obligation.
Examples:
Artistic creation
Martyrdom
Scientific curiosity
Pursuit of legacy
These behaviors appear difficult to reduce entirely to self-preservation or social obligation.
Position B: Meaning is derivative.
Meaning emerges from combinations of other motivational systems.
Perhaps meaning reflects:
anticipated social recognition,
internalized obligations,
cognitive coherence,
or long-term adaptive organization.
If so, meaning should not be regarded as a separate motivational system.
Questions
Could meaning simply be symbolic valuation?
Why do humans willingly sacrifice survival for symbolic ideals?
Is existential meaning uniquely human?
