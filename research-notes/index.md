# Research Notes

Research Notes contains preliminary philosophical observations, conceptual sketches, questions, and developing arguments.

Unlike working papers, these notes are exploratory. They document ideas under development before they are expanded into full philosophical essays.

---

## Current Notes

### 1. Motivation and the Is–Ought Distinction

Exploring whether motivational explanations can remain entirely descriptive without making normative claims. Can we describe how humans are motivated without implying how they ought to be motivated?

### 2. Primary vs Secondary Motivational Systems

Developing clearer criteria for distinguishing motivational systems that directly prescribe behavior from psychological factors that modify motivational weighting. What makes a system "primary"?

### 3. Meaning as a Primary Motivation

Examining whether meaning should be regarded as irreducible or explained through other motivational structures. Is meaning fundamental or derivative?

### 4. Motivational Competition in Extreme Environments

Notes and observations inspired by historical case studies such as the Kolyma labor camps. How do extreme conditions reshape motivational competition?
