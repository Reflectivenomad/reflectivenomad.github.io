# Motivation and the Is–Ought Distinction
Motivation and the Is–Ought Distinction
Research Question
Can motivational theory remain entirely descriptive?
Problem
Many motivational theories unintentionally slide into ethical conclusions.
For example:
"If humans are motivated by X, then humans ought to pursue X."
This inference appears invalid.
Proposed Position
The framework aims to remain descriptive.
It attempts to explain
how motivational systems compete,
how they are modified,
and how behavior emerges,
without prescribing which motivations should prevail.
The theory therefore remains compatible with diverse ethical systems.
Philosophical Connections
Hume's Is–Ought distinction
Moore's Naturalistic Fallacy
Nietzsche's genealogy
Evolutionary ethics
Questions
Can any motivational theory remain completely value-neutral?
Does describing motivation inevitably privilege some motivations?
At what point does psychology become ethics?
