# Reflective Nomad

*Independent Philosophy Research & Working Papers*

---

## Welcome

Reflective Nomad is an independent philosophy research workspace dedicated to developing, testing, and refining philosophical ideas.

Rather than presenting finished conclusions, this site serves as a **living archive** of working papers, research notes, and ongoing inquiry.

---

## Current Papers

### Motivational Competition: A Philosophical Proposal

Explores how multiple primary motivations simultaneously compete to produce a single realized behavior, introducing a framework of three primary motivational systems (self-preservation, duty, and meaning).

**[Read Paper →](motivational-competition.md)**

### Motivational Analysis of the Kolyma Camps

Applies the motivational framework to Varlam Shalamov's accounts of the Kolyma labor camps, examining how the framework explains human behavior under conditions of extreme deprivation.

**[Read Paper →](kolyma-analysis.md)**

## Current Research

### Interpretive Motivational Systems Framework (IMSF)

**Objective:** To develop a descriptive philosophical account of how competing motivations produce a single realized behavior.

**Current Stage:** Working Paper (Version 0.1)

**Current Questions:**
- Are there exactly three primary motivational systems?
- Is the distinction between primary and secondary systems coherent?
- How should motivational weighting be understood?
- Which existing philosophical theories offer the strongest objections?
